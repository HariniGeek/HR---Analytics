# HR Analytics Dashboard – Employee Attrition Analysis (Power BI)

---

## Problem Statement

Employee attrition is a critical challenge for organizations, impacting productivity, cost, and overall team stability.

This project aims to analyze employee data to:

* Identify key drivers of attrition
* Understand workforce behavior and patterns
* Provide actionable insights to improve employee retention

---

## Objectives

* Analyze overall attrition trends
* Identify factors influencing employee turnover
* Understand patterns across departments, roles, and demographics
* Enable data-driven HR decision-making

---

## Tools & Technologies

* Power BI – Dashboard development & visualization
* DAX – Calculated measures and KPIs
* Excel / CSV – Data source
* Data Modeling – Relationships and transformations

---

## Dashboard Overview

The dashboard is divided into three key sections:

---

### 1. Overview Dashboard

Provides a high-level summary of workforce and attrition trends.

**Key Highlights:**

* Total Employees, Attrition Count, Attrition Rate
* Attrition by Department, Job Role, Age, and Gender
* Interactive filters for dynamic exploration

---

### 2. Attrition Analysis (Key Drivers)

Focuses on identifying the root causes of attrition.

**Key Insights:**

* Higher attrition observed in **lower salary ranges**
* Majority attrition occurs within **early years of tenure (1–5 years)**
* Employees with **low job satisfaction** are more likely to leave
* Poor **work-life balance** significantly impacts retention
* Overtime contributes to increased attrition risk

---

### 3. Employee Insights (Segmentation Analysis)

Provides deeper analysis of workforce segments.

Key Insights:

Attrition is concentrated in specific job roles
Employees with lower compensation relative to experience show higher attrition
Attrition patterns vary across marital status groups
Combination of job role and low satisfaction increases attrition risk
Variations observed across department and gender segments

### Key Learnings

* Attrition analysis requires rate-based metrics, not just counts
* Combining multiple factors provides better insights than isolated analysis
* Visualization design plays a critical role in storytelling and decision-making
